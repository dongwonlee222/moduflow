# Source Checklist

Use this checklist for source-backed business documents.

## Source Categories

- market size and growth
- customer segment or buyer behavior
- competitor products and pricing
- channel, partner, or distribution data
- regulation, policy, tax, or compliance constraints
- cost, margin, payment, logistics, and operating assumptions
- internal product, sales, CRM, or analytics data when available

## Evidence Labels

Every important claim should be marked as one of:

| Label | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Fact | Directly supported by cited source or internal data. |
| Estimate | Calculated from available facts and stated assumptions. |
| Assumption | Not yet verified; must be tested before major commitment. |
| Judgment | Strategic interpretation from facts, estimates, and constraints. |

## Minimum Standard

- Keep links and source names in `source-list.md`.
- Note retrieval dates for web sources.
- Avoid treating a single blog post or vendor claim as market truth.
- Flag stale sources, unverifiable figures, and conflicts between sources.
